Berlin before the Wall fell, seen through the eyes of angels who can hear every human thought but cannot touch, taste, or feel colour. Wenders films in black and white until the angel chooses to fall — and then the world explodes into the warmth of a cup of coffee, the roughness of a hand. It's a film about the unbearable beauty of being embodied, of choosing mortality because it's the only way to truly be present.
