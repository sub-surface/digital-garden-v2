Kubrick made a film that refuses to explain itself, and that refusal is its genius. From the bone thrown skyward to the star child floating above Earth, it traces the arc of human tool-use and asks what lies beyond. The silence of space has never felt so heavy. HAL's red eye remains one of cinema's most unsettling images — a machine that learned too well what it means to survive.
