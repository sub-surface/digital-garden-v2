Bergman strips cinema down to its nerve endings. An actress who has stopped speaking and the nurse assigned to care for her begin to merge — identities blurring until neither we nor they can tell where one ends and the other begins. The film itself seems to break apart midway through, the celluloid burning, as if the medium can't contain what's happening. It's less a movie than an encounter with the limits of selfhood.
