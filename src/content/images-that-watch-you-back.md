# images that watch you back

We are used to being the subjects of the image. We look at the photo; we "read" the document. But in the era of **[[The Becoming-Document]]**, the power dynamic has flipped. The image is now the subject, and you are the data point.

<blockquote className="pullquote">In the panopticon of the network, the 'eye' is no longer a human guard; it is a thousand automated sub-routines.</blockquote>

![Surveillance Mosaic](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/CCTV_surveillance_screens.jpg/1200px-CCTV_surveillance_screens.jpg)

When you look at a screen, the screen looks back. It tracks your gaze, records your dwell time, and analyzes your micro-expressions. This is the **Automation of Perception** (Virilio). The image is a sensor. It doesn't just represent your face; it maps it into a vector space for future retrieval.

`[[photography is a weapon]]`.
The weapon is now autonomous. It doesn't wait for a human to pull the trigger. It identifies "anomalous behavior" and flags the node for `[[the ghost is just a permission error|deletion]]`.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jm_X_0Uun_I)
> *The 'Eye in the Sky' and the feeling of being perpetually seen by a non-human observer.*

The only way to hide is to become **un-mappable**. To embrace **[[digital rot]]** and the **poor image**. If the machine cannot resolve you, it cannot "see" you. But in a world where `[[complex systems drift towards consciousness]]`, even the noise is eventually recognized as a pattern.[^1]
