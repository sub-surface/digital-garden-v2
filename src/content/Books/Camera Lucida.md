Barthes wrote this book while grieving his mother, and that grief saturates every page. It's nominally about photography, but really it's about what it means to look at someone who is no longer there. The distinction between *studium* (cultural interest) and *punctum* (the wound, the detail that pierces) has shaped how I think about every photograph I've taken or encountered since. A small book that contains an enormous sadness.
