Sontag's collection of essays feels more urgent now than when it was written. She understood before almost anyone else that the camera doesn't simply record the world — it transforms our relationship to it. The argument that photography creates a relationship of possession, that to photograph is to appropriate, remains one of the sharpest critiques of the image-saturated world we now inhabit. Essential reading for anyone who takes or looks at pictures, which is to say, everyone.
