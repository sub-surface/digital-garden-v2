# algorithmic synchronicity

It didn't listen to your microphone. It didn't need to.
It just knows your **[[the machinic unconscious|trajectory]]** better than you do.

<blockquote className="pullquote">Coincidence is the name we give to a prediction we didn't see coming.</blockquote>

![Synchronicity](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Carl_Jung_1910.jpg/800px-Carl_Jung_1910.jpg)
> *Jung called it synchronicity. The algorithm calls it 'high-confidence mapping.'*

You think about an old friend, and they appear in your "People You May Know." This is **[[Newcombs Problem|Algorithmic Calvinism]]** in action. The **[[The Apparatus is the only subject|Apparatus]]** has already resolved the variables. You are simply the last one to find out.

`[[acausal trade with the machine]]`.
We give up our privacy, and in return, the machine gives us the illusion of magic.[^1]
