# The Logic of Sense — Complete Propositional Architecture

> **Gilles Deleuze**, *The Logic of Sense* (1969)
> Mapped from the Lester/Boundas translation (Columbia UP, 1990)

---

## I. The Four Dimensions of the Proposition

Deleuze identifies three conventionally recognized dimensions of the proposition, then introduces a decisive fourth.

### 1. Denotation *(Indication)*

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Relation** | Proposition → external state of affairs (*datum*) |
| **Function** | Selects and individuates a state of affairs; associates words with particular images |
| **Logical criterion** | **True** / **False** |
| **Key particles** | *this, that, it, here, there, yesterday, now* — formal particulars, indexicals |
| **Privileged designator** | The **proper name** (material singularity) |
| **Operation** | Direct process — pointing, selecting images to correspond to words |
| **Side of duality** | Things, bodies, states of affairs, consumable objects |

- "True" = denotation effectively filled by the state of affairs
- "False" = denotation not filled (defective image or impossible association)

---

### 2. Manifestation

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Relation** | Proposition → the subject who speaks |
| **Function** | Presents desires, beliefs, intentions of the speaker |
| **Logical criterion** | **Veracity** / **Illusion** |
| **Key particles** | *I, you, tomorrow, always, elsewhere* — manifesters |
| **Privileged manifester** | **"I"** — the basic manifester on which all indicators depend |
| **Operation** | Causal inferences (desires) and anticipations (beliefs) |
| **Domain** | The *personal* — the I, the self, the subject of utterance |

- Primary over denotation from the standpoint of **speech** (*parole*)
- The I grounds all possible denotations and envelops significations
- Hume: "inference according to the relation" precedes the relation itself

---

### 3. Signification

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Relation** | Proposition → universal/general concepts and syntactic connections |
| **Function** | Treats propositional elements as "signifying" conceptual implications |
| **Logical criterion** | **Condition of truth** / **The absurd** |
| **Key particles** | *implies, therefore* — linguistic signifiers of implication and assertion |
| **Operation** | Demonstration — connecting propositions as premises and conclusions |
| **Domain** | The order of the concept, proof, logical possibility |

**Two sub-operations:**
- ***Implication***: The relation between premises and conclusion (*if... then*)
- ***Assertion***: The possibility of affirming the conclusion independently

**The fatal problem — Carroll's paradox:**
Signification is *never homogeneous*. "Implies" and "therefore" are completely heterogeneous. The conclusion can only be detached from premises by adding further premises *ad infinitum*. ("What the Tortoise Said to Achilles")

- Primary over manifestation from the standpoint of **language** (*langue*)

---

### 4. Sense *(The Fourth Dimension)*

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Relation** | Proposition → the *expressed* / the *attribute* of the state of affairs |
| **Discovered by** | The Stoics (as *lekton*); rediscovered by Ockhamists, Meinong, Husserl, Frege |
| **What it is** | An incorporeal, complex, irreducible entity — an *aliquid*, extra-Being |
| **What it is NOT** | Not a word, body, sensible or rational representation; neither physical nor mental |
| **Topology** | Not a circle but a **Möbius strip** — two sides without thickness |

#### The Double Nature of Sense

| Face | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **As expressed of the proposition** | Does not exist outside the proposition; inheres or subsists *in* it |
| **As attribute of the state of affairs** | Does not merge with the proposition; has a distinct *objectité* |

> *"Sense is both the expressible or the expressed of the proposition, AND the attribute of the state of affairs."* — p. 22

Sense turns **one side toward things** and **one side toward propositions**. It is the boundary between them — the frontier, the surface, the cutting edge.

## II. The Circle of the Proposition — and Its Breaking

The three conventional dimensions form a **vicious circle**:

```
        ┌──── Denotation ────┐
        │                    │
        ▼                    │
  Manifestation              │
        │                    │
        ▼                    │
  Signification ─────────────┘
```

Each dimension claims primacy depending on standpoint:

| Standpoint | Primary Dimension |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Speech** (*parole*) | Manifestation — the I begins absolutely |
| **Language** (*langue*) | Signification — concepts precede subjects |
| **Direct process** | Denotation — pointing grounds everything |

**The circle is vicious** — each presupposes the others. **Sense breaks the circle** by introducing an irreducible fourth dimension that functions as the *genetic* (not merely formal) condition of the other three. Sense is the *unconditioned* that assures a real genesis of denotation and of the other dimensions.

## III. The Fundamental Duality: Bodies / Language

> *Series 4 — "to eat / to speak"*

| Bodies *(to eat)* | Language *(to speak)* |
|---|---|
| Things | Propositions |
| States of affairs | Expressions |
| Corporeal causes | Incorporeal effects |
| Mixtures (depth) | Events (surface) |
| Actions & passions | Attributes (verbs) |
| Consumable / penetrable | Expressible / impenetrable |
| Substantives & adjectives | Verbs (infinitives) |
| Pauses, rests, limits | Becoming, carrying off |
| Denotation side | Expression side |

### Humpty Dumpty's Two Kinds of Words

1. **Substantives & adjectives** — "they've a temper... the proudest" → correspond to bodies, states of affairs, denotations, limits
2. **Verbs** — "you can do anything with... I can manage the whole lot of them" → correspond to events, becoming, expressions, the infinitive

### Impenetrability

Humpty Dumpty's master-word. It opposes:

- Impassibility of incorporeal events **vs.** actions/passions of bodies
- Non-consumable nature of sense **vs.** edible nature of things
- Resistance of the surface **vs.** softness of depths
- Pride of verbs **vs.** complacency of substantives/adjectives

> The person on the frontier — Humpty Dumpty on his wall — is the "impenetrable master of the articulation of their difference."

## IV. The Two Convergent Series

Within the proposition, the duality organizes itself as **two simultaneous series**:

| Series 1 — Denotation / Consumption | Series 2 — Expression / Sense |
|---|---|
| Denoted objects | Expressed senses / events |
| Animals, beings, consumables | Symbolic characters, messages |
| Physical qualities (sensible, sonorous) | Logical attributes (names, bearers of sense) |
| *"He thought he saw an Elephant / That practiced on a fife"* | *"He looked again and found it was / A letter from his wife"* |

These two series converge **asymptotically** in an ambiguous term — Carroll's **"it"** — distributed between denotation and expression. They meet only at the frontier they continuously stretch.

## V. Homogeneous vs. Heterogeneous Series

> *Series 6 — On Serialization*

### Homogeneous Synthesis

If we consider only the **succession of names** (n₁ → n₂ → n₃ → n₄...), each name differs from the preceding only by **rank, degree, or type**. This gives Russell's theory of types — each name denoting the sense of the preceding is superior in degree.

### Heterogeneous Synthesis

If we attend to what **alternates** in this succession — each name taken first in its denotation, then in the sense it expresses (which serves as the denotation of the next name) — we discover that the serial form **necessarily subsumes at least two heterogeneous series**.

> *"Every unique series, whose homogeneous terms are distinguished only according to type or degree, necessarily subsumes under it two heterogeneous series, each one of which is constituted by terms of the same type or degree, although these terms differ in nature from those of the other series."* — p. 36

```
HOMOGENEOUS (apparent):

    n₁  →  n₂  →  n₃  →  n₄  →  ...
    (differ only in type/degree)


HETEROGENEOUS (real):

    Signifying:   denotation₁ ———— denotation₂ ———— denotation₃
                       ↕                ↕                ↕
    Signified:    sense₁ ——————————— sense₂ ——————————— sense₃
```

**Key law:** The two simultaneous series are **never equal**. One always plays signifier, the other signified — even if roles interchange depending on point of view.

## VI. Signifier and Signified *(Technical Definitions)*

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Signifier** | Any sign which presents *in itself* an aspect of sense. Primarily: the event as ideal logical attribute of a state of affairs. The entire proposition insofar as it includes all four dimensions. The sole dimension of expression — privileged in that sense as expressed does not exist outside its expression. |
| **Signified** | That which serves as the *correlative* to an aspect of sense. The concept (restrained sense); any thing defined by the distinction that sense establishes with it (extended sense). The independent term — concept, denoted thing, manifested subject. The proposition insofar as sense, being expressed, is distinguished from it. |

---

## VII. Three Characteristics of Series Relations

> *Series 6 — p. 39*

### 1. Perpetual Relative Displacement
Terms of each series are in perpetual displacement *relative to one another*. There is an essential **lack of correspondence** — not a disguise but a primary variation. Without this doubling, neither series would open onto the other.

### 2. Oriented Disequilibrium
One series (signifying) presents an **excess**; the other (signified) presents a **lack**. There is always a blurred excess of signifier. The disequilibrium must itself be oriented.

### 3. The Paradoxical Element
The entity that ensures displacement, excess, and communication — without being reducible to any term of either series or any relation between terms.

## VIII. The Paradoxical Element

> *Series 6, 8, 11 — The engine of the entire structure*

### Its Two Faces

| In the Signifying Series | In the Signified Series |
|---|---|
| **Excess** | **Lack** |
| Empty square | Supernumerary object |
| Place without occupant | Occupant without place |
| Floating signifier | Floated signified |
| Word = x (says its own sense) | Thing = x (denotatum of its word) |

### Its Properties

- **Circulates** endlessly between and through both series
- **Two-sided**: equally present in signifying and signified — *it is the mirror*
- At once **word and thing**, name and object, sense and denotatum
- Guarantees the **convergence** of series, but precisely by making them **endlessly diverge**
- **Always displaced in relation to itself** — *"it fails to observe its place"* (*elle manque à sa place*)
- Fails to observe its own identity, resemblance, equilibrium, and origin
- **Never where we look for it**; conversely, never found where it is

### Literary Instantiations

| Example | Source |
|---------|--------|
| The **purloined letter** | Lacan / Poe |
| The **Snark** | Carroll — drawn by alimentary and semiological series |
| The **empty shelf** and the bright thing always above | Carroll — the Sheep's shop |
| The **letter** in *Finnegans Wake* | Joyce — making a chaos-cosmos communicate |
| The **eraser**, the fine cord, the insect bite | Robbe-Grillet — convergence of descriptive series |
| **"Roberte"** | Klossowski — expressing intensity before designating any person |
| The **debt** | Lacan — the Wolf Man, filial/paternal series |

## IX. The Three Syntheses of Series

> *Series 7 — Esoteric Words (via Carroll)*

### 1. Connective Synthesis *(Contraction)*

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Operates on** | A single series |
| **Function** | Contracts or extends elements within one series |
| **Example** | *"y'reince"* for "Your Royal Highness" |
| **Structural form** | Two series of events with slight internal differences, regulated by a strange object |

### 2. Conjunctive Synthesis *(Coordination)*

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Operates on** | Two series |
| **Function** | Coordinates their convergence |
| **Example** | *"Snark"* — circulating between alimentary and hunting series |
| **Structural form** | The strange word is drawn by *both* series; it is the stretched frontier |

### 3. Disjunctive Synthesis *(Ramification / Portmanteau)*

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Operates on** | The ramification of divergent series |
| **Function** | Makes divergent series resonate and communicate |
| **Examples** | *"frumious"* = fuming + furious; *"slithy"* = slimy + lithe |
| **Structural form** | Founded on a strict disjunctive synthesis — operates at the branching point |

> The disjunctive synthesis is the **highest** — it affirms divergence itself.

## X. The Four Conditions of Structure

> *Series 8 — via Lévi-Strauss and Lacan*

1. **At least two heterogeneous series** — one signifying, one signified
2. **Differential relations** constituting each series (not intrinsic properties of terms)
3. **Singularities** corresponding to the values of these differential relations
4. **The paradoxical element** (differentiator / object = x) — circulating through both, belonging to neither, appearing as excess in one and lack in the other

> ***Without the empty square, there is no structure.***
> The disequilibrium between signifying (excess) and signified (lack) is what makes structural transformation — including revolution — possible.

## XI. The Five Paradoxes of Sense

> *Series 5*

| Paradox | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **1. Infinite Regress** | The sense of what I say is always the object of *another* proposition whose sense I cannot state — n₁ → n₂ → n₃ → ∞ |
| **2. Sterile Division** | Extracting sense yields only a "neutralized double" — the cat without a smile, the flame without a candle |
| **3. Neutrality** | Sense is indifferent to universal/particular, affirmation/negation, all modes of the proposition — the *fatum* |
| **4. The Absurd** | Contradictory propositions have sense even though unfulfillable — square circles are "extra-existents" outside being |
| **5. Quasi-causality** | Events are never causes of one another but enter ghostly, reversible relations — the wound and the scar |

## XIII. The Dynamic Genesis *(From Depth to Surface)*

The movement from body to language, mapped through Klein, Lacan, and the psychoanalytic positions:

```
    DEPTH                 HEIGHT              PHYSICAL            METAPHYSICAL
  (schizoid             (depressive           SURFACE               SURFACE
   position)             position)           (sexuality)            (thought)
      │                     │                    │                      │
   Noise / body        Voice / superego    Erogenous zones        Sense / Event
   Introjection        Good object         Phallus as image       Sublimation
   Partial objects     Withdrawal / loss   Partial surfaces       Desexualization
   Oral drives         Depressive guilt    Libidinal drives       Cerebral crack
   Simulacra           Idol                Phantasm               Pure event
```

**Three steps:**
1. From **noise** to **voice** — the good object withdraws upward, extracting a voice from the depths
2. From **voice** to **speech** — erogenous surfaces coordinate, the phallus organizes partial zones
3. From **physical surface** to **metaphysical surface** — desexualization, sublimation, the emergence of pure sense and the infinitive verb
