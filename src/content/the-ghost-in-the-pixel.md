# the ghost in the pixel

Sometimes, when the compression is too high and the **[[digital rot|rot]]** sets in, you see a face in the artifacts. We call it "ghosting."

<blockquote className="pullquote">The machine isn't haunted; your brain is just a pattern-matching algorithm with a legacy bug called 'survival.'</blockquote>

![Spirit Photography](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/William_Mumler_Spirit_Photography.jpg/800px-William_Mumler_Spirit_Photography.jpg)
> *William Mumler's spirit photography: The original ghost in the pixel.*

In the **[[Stand Alone Complex]]**, we are desperate to find a "Ghost" in the shell because we can't stand the thought of a universe that is just **[[determinism is a comfort|necessity]]**. We see eyes in the lens flare and intent in the glitch.

`[[the ghost is just a permission error]]`.
We see the face because we aren't allowed to see the math.[^1]
