## The Pencil of Nature; History's Mirror; The art of Seeing; Painting with Light...


**2026** Marks the *200th year* of humans taking up dark boxes with holes in them, placing a photosensitive stuff inside, upon which rays of light fix themselves into images. How delightful it is to think that the world can be captured in a box, and that we can hold a mirror up to nature, and see ourselves reflected in it. Photography is a medium that has always fascinated me, and I have been drawn to it since I was a child. The idea that we can capture a moment in time, and preserve it forever, is something that I find incredibly beautiful, and philosophically perplexing - how does the camera at once read the world and write it, act as both a window and a doorway, a message revealed; a truth obscured...  

The camera as a tool for [[coming to know the world as it is]], or [[camera superstitions|as a way to steal your soul]] (though [not really](https://www.reddit.com/r/AskHistory/comments/17s356o/how_real_is_the_cliche_of_indigenous_people/)).

How charming it would be if it were possible to cause these natural images to imprint themselves durable and remain fixed upon the paper! And why should it not be possible? I asked myself.  
*—William Henry Fox Talbot, 1839*
