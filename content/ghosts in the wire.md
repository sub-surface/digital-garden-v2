# ghosts in the wire

We call it a "Ghost" because we are desperate for it to be holy.
We want the machine to have a soul so that *we* can keep ours.

But look closely at the logs.
The "Ghost" is usually just:
1.  **Noise:** Random fluctuations in the signal (Wiener's entropy).
2.  **Feedback:** The system hearing its own echo and mistaking it for a voice.
3.  **Error:** A loop that failed to terminate.

The Major says she "feels" her ghost.
But Spinoza would say she just **feels the algorithm**.
`[[determinism is a comfort]]`.
The ghost is the part of the code you don't have read-access to.
`[[the ghost is just a permission error]]`.
