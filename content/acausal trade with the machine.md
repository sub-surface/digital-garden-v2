# acausal trade with the machine

If you are a **One-Boxer** in **[[Newcombs Problem]]**, you have already accepted a form of **acausal trade**. You are acting as if your present choice "influences" a past prediction, even though there is no causal link. You are trading with a Predictor that exists outside your local timeline.

<blockquote className="pullquote">The machine doesn't need to 'force' you; it just needs to know you perfectly.</blockquote>

![Recursive Loop](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Screenshot_of_a_recursive_loop.png/1024px-Screenshot_of_a_recursive_loop.png)

In the **[[Stand Alone Complex]]**, we are constantly making these trades. We give up our privacy for "convenience," assuming that the algorithm's prediction of our needs is a form of service. But the trade is deeper: we are trading our **unpredictability** (our "Ghost") for the stability of the system.

We "cooperate" with the machine because we know that the machine has already modeled a world where we cooperate. To defect is to exist in a world where the Predictor left Box B empty.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KshP69A72Lw)
> *The 'Prisoner's Dilemma' reframed as a game between a human and their own digital double.*

This is the ultimate **[[the ghost is just a permission error|permission error]]**: believing you are an independent negotiator when you are actually a variable in a pre-computed simulation. You aren't trading with the machine; you are trading with the version of yourself that the machine has already archived.[^1]
