# photography is a weapon

Virilio taught us that the camera and the gun share a lineage.
To *aim*. To *shoot*. To *capture*.

But in the age of the **[[The Becoming-Document|Becoming-Document]]**, the weaponization is no longer metaphorical.
The image is a **targeting vector**.

<blockquote className="pullquote">A photograph is no longer a memory; it is a set of coordinates for a drone.</blockquote>

![Targeting](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Target_Icon.svg/1024px-Target_Icon.svg.png)

When you upload a photo, you are not sharing a moment; you are **donating data** to the global surveillance apparatus. You are refining the machine's ability to recognize, categorize, and eventually, neutralize.

`[[the decisive moment is dead]]`.
The only moment that matters now is the one where the algorithm decides you are a threat.
`[[images that watch you back]]`.[^1]
