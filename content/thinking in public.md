# Writer's bloc; 

## *One or Several Swarms?**

In a post-truth, post-representation, and post-identity world, why are we so inundated with assertive claims of all three? 
- CEOs valiantly claim their sycophantic chatbots are `Truth-Seeking` by default (I'm looking at you , Elon); 
- Activist organisations and grassroots campaigners earn, through broken limbs and jail sentences, their media moments; 
- Thousands of name-less, face-less individuals find their communities in siloed digital spaces, safely air-gapped from the social contamination of the *outside world.* 
Might it be that the end of history was itself an historical moment, soon to be washed away like the naivete of a first love, which seems only more folly as time and experience sobers our collective hearts? Perhaps, perhaps not.

Are these the *white-man's laments* which Braidotti identifies as, "*the melancholia of privilage*" which beset so many critiques of contemporary society? The death throws of a wavering patriarchy? The Swan Song of a fading empire? I suspect not.[^1]

Why not? Well, that's why we're here, *you and I,* to find out why the world, history, progress, the whole shebang hasn't actually gone up in flames yet. With God-Emperor Donald at the helm of a new war in the middle east, only time will tell the shape of us on the other side of [[Photography|history's mirror]].
