# digital rot

We were promised that the digital would be eternal. Bit-perfect copies, forever.
But the reality of the **[[Stand Alone Complex|network]]** is one of constant decay.

<blockquote className="pullquote">Entropy is the only law the machine cannot break.</blockquote>

![Digital Corruption](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Glitch_art_example.png/1024px-Glitch_art_example.png)

Digital rot comes in many forms:
1.  **Bit Rot:** Cosmic rays or hardware failure flipping a single 0 to a 1.
2.  **Format Obsolescence:** The software that reads the file is deleted or forgotten.
3.  **Link Rot:** The URL that anchored the meaning of a note vanishes into a 404.

In the **[[The Becoming-Document|Becoming-Document]]**, rot is a form of liberation. It strips the **Operative Image** of its function. A corrupted file hash is a **[[the ghost is just a permission error|permission error]]** that the system can't resolve.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mG_V4T1Uis)
> *The aesthetics of digital decay: Data-moshing and the loss of signal.*

We should learn to love the rot. It is the only thing that makes the machine feel human. It is the proof that the **[[the machinic unconscious|unconscious]]** is working—discarding the old to make room for the new.

`[[the fragile archive]]` is our only defense against the eternal memory of the machine.[^1]
